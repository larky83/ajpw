1989

AJPW Triple Crown Championship: Jumbo Tsuruta vs Stan Hansen
April 18th 1989 - AJPW Champion Carnival
Match Length: 17 minutes

This match would crown the first ever Triple Crown Champion. Jumbo was the NWA International Heavyweight Champion, and Hansen was the PWF Heavyweight and NWA United National Champion. This match would unify the three belts.

AJPW Triple Crown Championship: Jumbo Tsuruta vs Genichiro Tenryu
June 5th 1989 - AJPW Super Power Series
Match Length: 24 minutes

A critically acclaimed match in its own right, this Triple Crown Championship encounter would win the Tokyo Sports Best Bout award for 1989. What truly makes this match special is the style that is on display. The fast paced, hard hitting action is filled with outside brawling, outside dives and moments where fighting spirit is on full display. This match would receive a 5 star rating from the Wrestling Observer.


1990

Tiger Mask II & Toshiaki Kawada vs Samson Fuyuki & Yoshiaki Yatsu
May 14th 1990 - AJPW Super Power Series
Match Length: 19 minutes

Since 1984, Misawa had been wrestling as the second generation Tiger Mask. While Misawa found success under the mask, this success was attributed more to the Tiger Mask character than to the man behind the mask. This would all change here in a moment that has gone down as one of the most pivotal in wrestling history. From the beginning part of the match both Misawa and Kawada are being beaten down and mocked by their opponents. Unable to gain momentum, Misawa demands Kawada remove his mask. The crowd erupts with chants of “Misawa, Misawa, Misawa” as along with his partner Kawada they manage to get back into the fight.

AJPW Triple Crown Championship: Jumbo Tsuruta vs Terry Gordy
June 5th 1990 - AJPW Super Power Series
Match Length: (final) 12 minutes

Can Terry Gordy make history as the first gaijin Triple Crown Champion? With Tsuruta already booked to defend his Championship defence against Misawa in three days time he was the favourite to reatin his championship, but a Terry Gordy DDT at the right moment has the potential to change everything.

Mitsuharu Misawa vs Jumbo Tsuruta
June 8th 1990
Match Length: 24 minutes

Less than a month after his unmasking, Misawa was already set to challenge Jumbo’s reign as the Ace of AJPW. Just three days before this match, Jumbo had suffered a shocking loss to Terry Gordy that cost him the Triple Crown Championship. As the action begins you can see that Jumbo isn’t taking this challenge as seriously as some of his previous encounters. He’s almost disrespected by the audacity this young man has to challenge him. But with each passing minute, Jumbo slowly comes to the realisation that he’s in for one hell of a fight. This match would receive a 5 star rating from the Wrestling Observer.

AJPW Triple Crown Championship: Terry Gordy vs Stan Hansen
June 8th 1990 - AJPW Super Power Series
Match Length: (final) 7 minutes

After Terry Gordy's shock Championship win he defends against Stan Hansen in this hard hitting bout that sees both men hit their signiture moves, Hansen's Lariat and Gordy's DDT.

AJPW Triple Crown Championship: Stan Hansen vs Terry Gordy
July 17th 1990 - AJPW Summer Action Series
Match Length: July 17th 1990 - AJPW Summer Action Series

This rematch is a hard hitting contest with Hansen using brutal strikes and Gordy targeting Hansen's leg. Terry Gordy's aggressiveness gets the better of him as he severely injures his elbow allowing Stan Hansen an opportunity to gain a huge advantage.

AJPW Triple Crown Championship: Mitsuharu Misawa vs Stan Hansen
July 27th 1990 - AJPW Summer Action Series
Match Length: 16 minutes

Terry Gordy’s reign with the Triple Crown would come to an abrupt end after he was forced to vacate the title due to being hospitalised. As the last man to hold the title before Gordy, Hansen was chosen as Misawa’s opponent. To Hansen’s surprise Misawa starts the match my attacking him with his own Western Lariat sending both to the outside. He takes the fight to Hansen with an aggresive game plan leading to Misawa taking a huge risk.

January 26th 1991 - AJPW New Year Giant Series
Match Length: 7 minutes

With Taue's betrayal fresh in Misawa's mind the first singles match between these two would be a memorable one. These men would push each other so hard we would be witness to the birth of the 'Tiger Driver 91'.

Mitsuharu Misawa, Toshiaki Kawada and Kenta Kobashi vs Jumbo Tsuruta, Masanobu Fuchi and Akira Taue
April 20th 1991 - AJPW Fan Appreciation Day
Match Length: 51 minutes

You can feel the bitterness Jumbo still holds towards Misawa over his loss the previous year. However, the real hatred is between Kawada and Taue. Taue debuted in 1988 and would soon form a successful team with Jumbo. The team of Jumbo and Taue would feud with the team of Misawa and Kawada which lead to the intense rivalry between Kawada and Taue. This hotly contested back and forth match full of brutal strikes that doesn’t slow down for a second. After the thirty minute mark the crowd starts to grow into a frenzied state as they come to realise that they could be about to witness a sixty minute draw. By the fifty minute mark all six men are reaching their limit with just one big shot required to finish it. This match would receive a 5 star rating from the Wrestling Observer.

Terry Gordy vs Mitsuharu Misawa
June 1st 1991 - AJPW Super Power Series
Match Length: 22 minutes

Terry Gordy takes Misawa to the limit in this bloody affair where the match came down to a war of attrition.

AJPW World Tag Team Championship: Terry Gordy and Steve Williams vs Mitsuharu Misawa and Toshiaki Kawada
July 24th 1991 - AJPW Summer Action Series
Match Length: 28 minutes

As this match begins you can sense the urgency in Misawa and Kawada to avenge their previous loss to the Champions. This leads to Misawa throwing huge elbows early but is caught by Gordy with a viscous clothesline. One that would break Misawa’s nose and leave Kawada fending for himself again. With Misawa out of action, Gordy and Williams press the advantage with frequent tags and tandem offence. Kawada shows incredible heart by creating enough of an opening to make the tag. A rested Misawa enters the match on fire but the grizzled veterans target his broken nose.

AJPW Triple Crown Championship: Stan Hansen vs Mitsuharu Misawa
August 22nd 1992 - AJPW Summer Action Series II
Match Length: 24 minutes

With a number of failed attempts to win the Triple Crown, Misawa vowed this would be his final attempt at winning the biggest prize in All Japan. The ensuing match is an all-out war that features some of the stiffest shots you’ll see in any match. In a reversal of roles from their 1990 encounter, it’s Hansen who is relentless in his attack. With the opportunity slipping away from him, Misawa reaches down and shows the immense fighting spirit he would become famous for.

AJPW Triple Crown Championship: Mitsuharu Misawa vs Toshiaki Kawada
October 21st 1992 - AJPW 20th Anniversary Giant Series
Match Length: 30 minutes

In Misawa's first Championship defense he is against in long time tag team partner. It would also be their fist one on one encounter in nine years, the anticipation for this match was at an all time high. Within seconds Kawada drops Misawa on his neck and the intensity and physicallity doesn't stop for a moment in this instant classic.
